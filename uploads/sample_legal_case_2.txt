IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE

Johnson vs Union of India

Facts of the Case:
The petitioner Johnson was employed under a government contract for a fixed term.
The contract was terminated before the completion period without prior notice.
The petitioner claimed wrongful termination and loss of livelihood.

Arguments by the Petitioner:
It is argued that the termination violated contractual terms.
The petitioner contends that principles of natural justice were ignored.

Arguments by the Respondent:
The respondent submits that the termination was due to policy changes.
It was claimed that the contract allowed termination without notice.

Judgment:
After reviewing the contractual clauses, the court observed procedural lapses.
The court partially ruled in favor of the petitioner and directed compensation.

Precedents Referred:
Smith vs State
Kumar vs Government of India
